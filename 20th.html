Coal Chronicles: The Rise of Fossil Fuels

Step into the mid-20th century, a time when coal and other fossil fuels powered the world, driving massive industrial growth and technological advancements. This era, marked by the Industrial Revolution, saw coal become the main source of electricity and energy, fueling factories, trains, and homes. However, this rapid development came with significant environmental impacts that we still face today. In the bustling cities and expanding industries of the 20th century, coal was king. It powered the steam engines that drove the Industrial Revolution, transforming economies and societies. Factories were busy, trains traveled across continents, and homes were warmed by coal fires. But as the smoke rose and the skies darkened, the environmental consequences began to show. Air pollution became common, and early signs of climate change started to appear. Your mission is to understand the environmental impacts of fossil fuels. You will collect and arrange photos showing the use of coal and other fossil fuels during the 20th century, highlighting both the industrial advancements and the environmental damage.
